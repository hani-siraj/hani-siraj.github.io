Key Patterns / What Winners Share

Every Image is Clickable

In ALL top image emails, 100% of content images are linked. Zero decorative images. Every pixel is a click target, maximising clickable surface area.

Top 6 image emails: 100% images linked. Avg 7.3 shop links per email.

2-3 Products, No More

The sweet spot is 2-3 unique products. Single-product emails averaged 0.39% CTR. 3-product emails averaged 0.66% CTR. Too few = insufficient variety, too many = decision paralysis.

Top 3 by clicks all featured exactly 3 products.

Conversational Alt Text

Winners use lowercase, conversational image text: "mmhm...what's next?", "so...what are you wearing?". Emails with rich alt text averaged 0.65% CTR vs 0.32% for empty alt text.

Rich alt text = 2x the click rate of empty alt text.

Education Before Product

Highest-click emails lead with value (styling tips, occasion framing) before introducing products. Hook, educate/inspire, then "here's what to buy".

Styling Tips (educate first): 466 clicks. Game Day (product first): 23 clicks.

Scenario-Based Framing

Frame products around a scenario: "what are you wearing this valentine's?", "before your next headout". Aspirational storytelling, not product display.

Scenario emails avg $813 revenue vs display emails avg $579.

"Goes With" Styling Context

The highest-click email added "goes with: slides and oversized sunglasses" beneath each product. This micro-detail adds ~50% more clicks than the same template without it.

V-Day Self-Gift Guide: 479 clicks. Same series without "goes with": 300-311.
