The Brand

Hong Kong's #1 online premium meat and seafood retailer.

Steak King is a direct-to-consumer brand serving Hong Kong's expat and foodie community with premium cuts, including Australian Wagyu, USDA Prime, and Japanese A5. Their flagship Meat Emporium sale is the brand's biggest annual event.

I manage the full email and SMS lifecycle for Steak King, covering campaign strategy, copywriting, flow architecture, and performance reporting.

The Challenge

Drive a record-breaking sale with a single hero product and a tight window.

The March 2026 Meat Emporium sale ran for just eight days. The brief was clear: make it the biggest sale in the brand's history, anchored around a Rangers Station M3-4 Wagyu Ribeye at HK$298 (75% off retail).

With only 200 units of the hero product available at launch, I needed to generate maximum urgency and convert at scale before stock disappeared.

The Strategy

A three-phase lifecycle engine built on scarcity, sequencing, and mid-sale momentum.

Rather than a standard launch-and-reminder sequence, I architected a phased campaign strategy with distinct psychological triggers at each stage.

Phase 1 — Pre-Sale Hype
Hero product reveal and demand building
Two hype emails before launch. The first revealed the hero product (HK$298 Wagyu Ribeye) with a "this won't last" narrative. The second built sale-wide anticipation without discounting the hero further. Both designed to make the reader feel they were getting intelligence, not being sold to.
Phase 2 — Launch Day
HK$1.02M in revenue. One order every 29 seconds at peak.
The launch email dropped at 8AM HKT. Within hours, the hero product sold out completely. Peak performance hit HK$2,765 per minute with one order landing every 29 seconds. The email drove 34% of the day's revenue directly from the hero product alone. Total Day 1: HK$1,025,221 from 824 orders.
Phase 3 — Mid-Sale LMTs
Limited-time mechanics to sustain momentum after the hero sold out
With the hero gone, I deployed a series of Limited Mechanic Triggers: tiered Spend & Save offers, a "Beat Your Neighbour" social proof angle, and an exclusive Rangers Station extra 10% off. Each LMT ran for 24-48 hours only. LMT campaigns alone drove HK$257K+ in incremental revenue at an average AOV of HK$1,524.
Phase 4 — Restock & Close
200 more units. Sold out again.
On Day 7, a surprise restock of 200 Wagyu Ribeye units dropped at 8AM. The restock email generated immediate sell-through. Stock was pulled from the site at end of day regardless of remaining inventory, maintaining the scarcity narrative for future sales.

Key Takeaway

Lifecycle wasn't a support channel. It was the revenue engine.

Over 70% of the sale's total revenue was directly attributed to email. The eight-campaign sequence maintained a 0.11% unsubscribe rate while generating over HK$1.2M in email-attributed revenue. Every email had a distinct job in the sequence. Nothing was filler.

The hero product sold out twice in under 24 hours each time, with 1,361 total units moved across the sale. The scarcity playbook, combined with timed LMT mechanics, proved that lifecycle can be the primary revenue driver when the strategy is built around it, not bolted on after.
