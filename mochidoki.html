The Brand

New York's cult mochi ice cream company.

Mochidoki is a renowned New York-based mochi ice cream company, celebrated for its innovative and sophisticated Japanese-inspired desserts. Committed to using only the finest ingredients, their mission is "Connecting people by crafting moments of joy and discovery."

Challenges Faced

Four sales channels, one email list, and no strategy to connect them.

01
Multi-channel cannibalisation
Promoting B2B, DTC e-commerce, Amazon Fresh, and retail stores simultaneously without undermining each other. Every channel was competing for the same subscriber's attention.
02
$75 minimum order barrier
The DTC store had a $75 minimum order value, which meant not every subscriber was the right fit for an e-commerce push. Audiences needed to be routed to the right channel based on their likelihood to convert.
03
Zero segmentation or personalisation
Standard email campaigns lacked effective audience segmentation and personalisation. Every subscriber received the same generic message regardless of buying behaviour, location, or purchase history.

Solution 1

Enhanced B2B experience: frequency-based reorder flows.

I reclassified B2B users into six buckets based on their historical reorder frequency: 7, 14, 21, 30, 45, and 60-day intervals. Each bucket triggered a personalised automated flow timed to when that business customer was statistically likely to reorder, improving repeat rates and lifetime value.

Solution 2

Location-based targeting: dynamic widgets that routed users to the right channel.

I deployed RetainIQ dynamic widgets that targeted non-converting users based on their proximity to physical stores. If a subscriber was near a Mochidoki retail location, the email would route them there. If they were in an Amazon Fresh delivery zone, it pushed that instead. For subscribers who cleared the $75 minimum threshold, they were directed to the DTC store.

The goal was simple: convert the click into a sale through whichever channel had the lowest friction for that specific person.

Key Takeaway

Mochidoki didn't need more subscribers. They needed smarter routing.

By matching each subscriber to the right channel and the right cadence, the same list generated more than double the revenue with significantly higher engagement across the board. The B2B frequency flows turned sporadic wholesale orders into predictable revenue, and the location widgets solved the $75 minimum problem by simply sending people to the channel where they were most likely to buy.
