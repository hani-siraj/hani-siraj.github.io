About

Mochidoki is a renowned New York-based mochi ice cream company, celebrated for its innovative and sophisticated Japanese-inspired desserts. Committed to using only the finest ingredients, their mission is "Connecting people by crafting moments of joy and discovery."

Challenges faced

🛒
Promoting multiple sales channels simultaneously without undermining each other. B2B, B2C e-commerce, Amazon Fresh, and retail stores.
🎯
Overcoming a $75 minimum order value on the website and channelling the right audience to the right business channel.
✉
Standard email campaigns lacked effective audience segmentation and personalisation.

Solutions implemented

Enhanced B2B Experience

B2B customers accounted for a significant portion of online revenue, regularly reordering from the site. Previously, the brand segmented all B2B users and sent out re-order reminder campaigns three times a month. I saw an opportunity to optimise this and boost the re-order rate.

By classifying B2B users into six buckets based on their average reorder frequency (7, 14, 21, 30, 45, and 60 days), I set up an automated, personalised replenishment flow. This greatly improved repeat order rates, increased LTV, and enhanced the B2B customer experience.

Location-Based Targeting

I implemented location-based targeting using RetainIQ dynamic widgets to nudge users who weren't purchasing due to the $75 limit towards physical stores, retail partners, or Amazon Fresh.

I specifically targeted users who spent considerable time on the website but did not convert. If they were near a physical store, they would see dynamic creatives in the emails and flows directing them to that store. If not, they were encouraged to purchase from Amazon Fresh. This strategy effectively promoted all sales channels without cannibalising any single channel.
