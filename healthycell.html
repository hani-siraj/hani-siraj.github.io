The Brand

A science-backed supplement brand with a solid email foundation that needed sharper edges.

HealthyCell is a leading US-based health supplement brand known for its science-backed, high-quality products. They already had a functioning email programme, but wanted to push performance further through personalisation, better engagement, and stronger loyalty programme adoption.

The Challenge

Good email performance, but generic. Every subscriber saw the same thing.

HealthyCell needed to increase click rates, boost order volume, drive more subscription opt-ins, and strengthen their loyalty programme. The existing campaigns were competent but one-size-fits-all. There was no dynamic content, no behavioural targeting, and no personalisation beyond a first name merge tag.

The Strategy

Four personalisation layers, A/B tested across nine campaigns over 30 days.

I audited their entire email strategy through a personalisation lens and identified four high-impact opportunities.

Browse abandonment blocks in campaigns. Added dynamic widgets showing each subscriber's recently browsed or abandoned products directly inside marketing emails, turning every campaign into a personalised retargeting touchpoint.

Social proof hero images. Replaced generic hero banners with dynamically rendered product images relevant to each subscriber's browsing history, paired with social signals to build trust during new product launches.

Loyalty programme activation. Targeted subscribers sitting on unused reward points with specific messaging showing them how to redeem points on products they'd previously browsed, lowering the friction to try something new.

First-purchase trust building. For low-intent subscribers who hadn't converted, deployed social proof blocks with ratings, review counts, and a first-time buyer discount to reduce purchase anxiety.

The Results

Nine campaigns. 30 days. Every metric improved.

Key Takeaway

Personalisation doesn't need a platform overhaul. It needs the right lens.

HealthyCell already had good email infrastructure. The lift came from layering behavioural personalisation on top of what already existed. No migration, no new platform. Just smarter use of the data they were already collecting.
